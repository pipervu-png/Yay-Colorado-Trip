What We're Doing

Epic Colorado Adventures

From jaw-dropping hikes to soaking in natural hot springs — May is prime time in the Rockies.

🥾
Hiking

Rocky Mountain National Park

Over 350 miles of trails. Emerald Lake, Sky Pond, or Bear Lake — all iconic, all stunning. Snow may still cap the peaks.

🗓 Day 1–2 ⏱ All day 💪 Moderate
🌊
Rafting

Arkansas River White Water

May snowmelt means peak flows on the Arkansas. Royal Gorge and Browns Canyon offer everything from Class II to V rapids.

🗓 Day 3 ⏱ Half day 🌊 Thrilling
♨️
Hot Springs

Strawberry Park Hot Springs

Hidden in the Steamboat Springs forest, these natural pools are the ultimate after-hike wind-down. Rustic and magical.

🗓 Day 4 🌙 Evening 😌 Chill
🦌
Wildlife

Elk & Wildlife Watching

Moose, elk, bighorn sheep, and marmots are everywhere in May. Bring binoculars and patience — you'll see something incredible.

🗓 Any day 🌅 Dawn/Dusk 📷 Photo op
🚵
Biking

Vail Pass Bike Path

One of Colorado's most iconic paved trails — 17 miles from Vail Pass down to Copper Mountain with jaw-dropping alpine views the whole way.

🗓 Day 5 ⏱ 3–5 hrs 🚵 Fun
🍺
Nightlife

Dillon Dam Brewery + Breckenridge

Dillon Dam Brewery is a Summit County classic. Or head 20 min to Breckenridge's lively Main Street for dinner, bars, and live music.

🗓 Every evening 🌙 Evening 🍻 Social

The Schedule

Day by Day

A loose plan — enough structure to actually do things, enough flex for spontaneous detours.

1

Sunday — Arrival Day

Silverthorne — We Made It 🏔

Leave Matthew's at 4am. Long drive through the mountains, arrive mid-morning. Hit the grocery store to stock up, unpack, hydrate — altitude hits hard at 8,000 ft. Easy dinner at the house and an early night. Tomorrow starts the real trip.

🚗 4am departure 🛒 Grocery run 💧 Hydrate hard 🌙 Early night
2

Monday — Acclimation Day

Dillon Reservoir + Outlets 🛍️

Coffee with mountain views, then a slow lakeside walk around Dillon Reservoir — easy on the lungs while you adjust to altitude. Browse the Silverthorne Outlets in the afternoon. Chill evening back at the house: hot tub, games, good vibes.

☕ Mountain coffee 🚶 Lakeside walk 🛍️ Silverthorne Outlets 🛁 Hot tub night
3

Tuesday — Denver Day Trip

Denver — City Day 🦁

~1.5 hr drive down to Denver. Hit the Denver Zoo, Aquarium, or Museum — grab a CityPass to save on combo tickets. Big city energy after two mountain days. Drive back to Silverthorne in the evening.

🚗 1.5 hr drive 🦁 Denver Zoo 🐠 Aquarium 🎟️ CityPass
4

Wednesday — Recovery Day

Frisco — Slow Morning, Lake Views 🌊

Sleep in. Head over to Rainbow Lake and the Peaks Trailhead for an easy hike with big views. Brunch in Frisco then wander Main Street and the marina. Optional paddleboarding if the weather cooperates. Chill night back at the house.

😴 Sleep in 🌈 Rainbow Lake 🥞 Brunch in Frisco 🏄 Paddleboarding
5

Thursday — Breckenridge Day

Breckenridge — Mohawk Trail + Main Street 🏘️

Early morning up Mohawk Lakes Trail — waterfalls, wildflowers, and dramatic alpine basins. Head into Breckenridge for lunch and some shopping. Decide as a group if a second trail is in order, or cruise Boreas Pass Road for incredible views without hiking.

🥾 Mohawk Lakes Trail 🍔 Breck lunch 🛍️ Main Street 🚗 Boreas Pass Road
6

Friday

St. Mary's Glacier ❄️

One of Colorado's most accessible glaciers — a short but steep hike up to a year-round snowfield at 11,000 ft. Stunning views, possible snowball fights, and a real "we're in Colorado" moment. One of the trip highlights.

❄️ St. Mary's Glacier 🏔 11,000 ft 🌨️ Snowfield in May
7

Saturday — Flex Day

Your Call — Acorn Creek Trail 🌿

Last full day — the crew decides. Acorn Creek Trail is a great option: peaceful, scenic, and a perfect send-off hike. Or use the day to revisit a favorite spot, squeeze in something you missed, or just decompress before the drive home.

🌿 Acorn Creek Trail 🗳️ Group vote 🎒 Last adventure
